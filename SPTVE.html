Special Program in TVE

Fisheries-focused technical-vocational education for Junior High School

The Special Program in Technical-Vocational Education (SPTVE) equips Junior High School students with hands-on competencies in fisheries and food processing, giving them a head start in a specialized career or further academic studies.

SPTVE Specializations

Choose from three fisheries-centered tracks.

Fish Capture

NC I

Students learn responsible and sustainable fishing methods using modern and traditional gear. This track develops skills in boat handling, net operations, and fish handling at sea.

  • Operate and maintain fishing gear & equipment
  • Perform boat and vessel handling procedures
  • Apply responsible fishing practices
  • Handle and store freshly caught fish properly
  • Observe maritime safety regulations
NC I – Fish Capture

Fish Culture

NC II

This track focuses on aquaculture — the controlled breeding, rearing, and harvesting of fish and other aquatic organisms. Students manage fish ponds, hatcheries, and water quality.

  • Set up and manage fish ponds & cages
  • Breed and rear finfish and shellfish
  • Monitor and manage water quality parameters
  • Apply feed management and nutrition principles
  • Harvest and transport products
NC II – Aquaculture

Fish / Food Processing

NC II

Students master techniques for preserving, processing, and packaging fish and other food products. The track covers food safety, value-adding, and small enterprise management.

  • Cure, smoke, and dry fish products
  • Perform canning and bottling operations
  • Apply and follow food safety standards
  • Operate food processing equipment safely
  • Package and label products for market
NC II – Food Processing
