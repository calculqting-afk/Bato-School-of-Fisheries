About Our School

Building a legacy of learning and service since 1985.

Our Story

Bato School of Fisheries was established in 1985 in the municipality of Bato, Leyte. Originally focused on fisheries and aquaculture education, the school has grown into a full senior high school offering diverse academic and technical programs.

Today, BSF stands as one of the most respected secondary institutions in the region, known for its balance between technical-vocational excellence and academic achievement. We are committed to producing graduates who are globally competitive yet deeply rooted in Filipino values.

Located along Jose P. Rizal Street in Tinago, Bato, Leyte, our school continues to serve the youth of the community with dedication and passion.

Mission, Vision & Values

Mission

To provide accessible, relevant, and quality education that develops learners with strong technical skills, academic competence, and responsible citizenship.

Vision

A learner-centered school community that prepares globally competitive graduates rooted in service, integrity, and love for the environment.

Core Values

BSF upholds the DepEd core values of Maka-Diyos, Maka-tao, Makakalikasan, and Makabansa in school life and community service.

School Facilities

BSF provides modern, well-equipped spaces that support every aspect of student growth — academic, technical, and recreational.

Science Labs

Fully equipped biology, chemistry, and fisheries labs for hands-on learning and live experiments.

Library

Over 5,000 volumes plus digital resources accessible to all students for research and study.

Computer Rooms

Four computer labs with high-speed internet for ICT subjects, research, and digital projects.

Achievements & Recognition

BSF has consistently proven its excellence in academics, arts, sports, and technical education across division, regional, and national competitions.

Technical Skills Training

Hands-on fisheries and technical-vocational learning that prepares students for work, enterprise, and further studies.

Academic Participation

Students are encouraged to join academic activities, research tasks, and classroom-based competitions.

Student Leadership

Arts Development

The arts program supports student creativity through music, dance, visual arts, theater, media, and writing.
