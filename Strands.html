Senior High School Programs

Academic Track · Technical-Professional Track · ALS

All of these tracks will pave pathways that provide fundamental and essential courses that will help you excel in your desired profession in the future!

"Here at Bato School of Fisheries, we instill values in our students and bring the very best version of themselves!"

Choose Your Track

Bato School of Fisheries offers two tracks under the Senior High School curriculum plus ALS.

Academic Track

For students who plan to pursue college or university after Senior High School. Choose from three clusters based on your passion and career goal.

Arts, Social Science, and Humanities Cluster

For students passionate about communication, culture, social issues, the arts, and public service.

Communication Arts

Develop strong oral and written communication skills for professional and civic life.

Social Sciences

Study of Philippine history, politics, governance, sociology, and culture.

Humanities

Explore literature, philosophy, creative writing, and the fine arts.

Career Pathways

Law, Education, Journalism, Political Science, Psychology, Social Work, Communication Arts.

Business and Entrepreneurship Cluster

For students interested in business, finance, entrepreneurship, and management careers.

Accountancy & Finance

Fundamentals of bookkeeping, financial statements, and accounting principles.

Business Management

Business structures, planning, and managing people and resources effectively.

Entrepreneurship

Starting and managing a business, business planning, and financial literacy.

Career Pathways

Accountancy, Business Administration, Entrepreneurship, Marketing, Finance, Economics.

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM)

For students who love solving problems, conducting research, and exploring the natural world.

Biology & Chemistry

Cell biology, genetics, chemical reactions, and laboratory work for health and science careers.

Physics & Engineering

Motion, energy, waves, and electricity — essential for engineering and technology courses.

Advanced Mathematics

Pre-calculus, statistics, and discrete math for STEM fields.

Career Pathways

Medicine, Nursing, Engineering, Architecture, Computer Science, Biology, Veterinary Science.

🔧 Technical-Professional Track

Equips students with practical skills. Graduates are ready for employment, entrepreneurship, or higher education.

Agriculture and Fishery Arts

Our signature track rooted in the school's identity. Hands-on training in fisheries and food processing.

Fish Capture

Learn proper fish capture methods, gear handling, and sustainable fishing practices.

Fish Culture

Aquaculture, fish pond management, fingerling production, and marine resource management.

Fish/Food Processing

Food preservation, processing techniques, and product development with TESDA NC II.

Family and Consumer Science

Practical skills in food service, baking, and household management.

Bakery Operation

Bread, pastry, and cake production aligned with TESDA Bread and Pastry Production NC II.

Events Management Services

Planning, organizing, and executing events from small gatherings to large functions.

Food & Beverage Services

Restaurant and food service operations aligned with TESDA Food & Beverage Services NC II.

Kitchen Operations

Professional cooking techniques, kitchen management, and culinary arts fundamentals.

Industrial Arts

Hands-on technical training in metalwork and welding.

Manual Metal Arc Welding

Structural welding techniques, Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW)

Metal Fabrication

Creating metal structures and products used in construction and manufacturing industries.

Career Pathways

Welder, Fabricator, Construction Worker, Manufacturing Technician, and more.

Alternative Learning System (ALS)

Provides an alternative pathway for out-of-school youth and adults who were unable to complete their formal education.

What is ALS?

A parallel learning system that gives everyone a second chance to earn their Senior High School equivalency.

Who Can Enroll?

Out-of-school youth, adults, and anyone who cannot attend regular school due to work or family circumstances.

Flexible Schedule

Classes are built around the learner's availability, with modular and community-based learning options.

Accreditation

Completers receive a DepEd-recognized SHS certificate and can proceed to college and employment.

Learning Modes:

Face-to-face sessions, modular distance learning, and community learning centers.


ALS Assessment:

Learners take the Accreditation & Equivalency (A&E) Test administered by DepEd.


How to Enroll ALS?

Visit the BSF Admin office for ALS enrollment details.
